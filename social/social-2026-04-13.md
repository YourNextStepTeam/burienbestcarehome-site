I got a message last week from a woman in King County who said something that stuck with me.

"I've been Googling senior care costs at 2am for three weeks, and every time I close my laptop, I feel like I'm going to be sick."

She's not alone. In caregiver forums right now, the same question keeps coming up in different words: "How do people afford this?" It's asked with a kind of quiet desperation, like maybe everyone else figured out a secret that passed them by.

Here's what I wish someone had told this woman, and every family sitting at a kitchen table trying to make impossible numbers work: the fact that you can't easily afford senior care is not a personal failure. It is the structural reality of eldercare in America. Professional care services average over $100,000 a year. Assisted living in Washington runs $5,500 to $6,200 a month before memory care surcharges. Nursing homes can top $10,000 a month. These are not numbers that most families can absorb, no matter how carefully they planned.

And here's the part that breaks my heart: most families never hear about the option that changes the math entirely.

Adult family homes are licensed residential care homes with a maximum of six residents. The care ratio is 1:3 (compared to 1:10 or more in large facilities). The cost is typically 30 to 50 percent less than assisted living. And in Washington state, Medicaid covers adult family home care through the COPES waiver.

There are 21 licensed adult family homes right here in Burien. Your parent doesn't have to live in a 60-bed facility with rotating staff. They can live in a real home, with caregivers who know their name, in a community they recognize.

The money conversation is hard. It feels like you're reducing your parent's life to a spreadsheet. But I've learned something after 20 years in this work: the families who face the financial conversation early, even when it's uncomfortable, are the ones who find the best care. The avoidance is what leads to crisis decisions at $6,800 a month with no time to explore options.

You are not failing your parent by looking at the numbers. You are protecting them.

If your family is in the middle of this right now and you don't know where to start, read today's article on our site. No sales pitch. Just every option laid out clearly, including ones most families have never heard of: burienbestcarehome.com
