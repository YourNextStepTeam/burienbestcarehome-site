## 4. LINKEDIN POST (healthcare professionals + referral network)

**Suggested image concept:** A flat-lay of the Washington Connection COPES application next to a DSHS envelope, a pen across the corner, and an uncapped highlighter, with soft daylight and a small sage or terracotta accent (a cardigan folded at the corner of the desk, a small plant at the edge of frame). **Post copy:**

A resource note for King County elder law attorneys, geriatric care managers, hospital case managers, SNF discharge planners, and financial planners whose clients are in the private-pay runway for adult family home or assisted living care.

Three structural facts about Washington's COPES waiver that families still arrive to first meetings without knowing, and that a well-timed one-page handout at intake will compress several weeks of client anxiety into a single conversation.

First, Washington is structurally different from most states on Medicaid long-term care. The state made a deliberate policy choice decades ago to invest in community-based alternatives over institutional nursing homes, which is why Washington has roughly 3,000 licensed adult family homes (the highest per-capita concentration in the country, with 21 in Burien alone). COPES reimburses AFH placement at roughly $4,500 to $5,500 per month versus roughly $10,000 to $12,000 for a nursing home bed, so the state actively wants clients in adult family homes. Families who arrive framing their application as "settling" or "going on welfare" benefit from the infrastructure reframe very early.

Second, the Community Spouse Resource Allowance in 2026 is $162,660, the Minimum Monthly Maintenance Needs Allowance in Washington is approximately $2,555 per month (higher with documented shelter costs), and the community-spouse home exemption is in force while the well spouse occupies the home. Many families walk into the first meeting believing the community spouse will be impoverished by the application. A two-sentence explanation of the CSRA and MMMNA at intake resolves this.

Third, the 45-day processing window is the practical lever, not the eligibility rules. Eligibility is measured as of the application date, not the approval date, which makes the single highest-value client counseling action "file before the runway runs out, not after." The same action resolves the retroactive Medicaid question almost entirely.

At Burien Best Care Home we accept COPES placements and we review pending applications case by case. Happy to be a resource for colleagues working with families in the AFH pathway. Referral is straightforward, and I will say no directly if our home is not the right level of care for the client.

Becca Pitts
