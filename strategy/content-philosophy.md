# Burien Best Care Home — Content Philosophy

*The foundation beneath all content creation for this business.*

---

## The Origin Story
Becca is building the type of care she wishes she had for her own parents. This is not a marketing angle. This is the emotional truth that drives everything.

## First Principles (Irrefutable Truths)

**Premise 1:** Every family going through an eldercare transition is experiencing grief, even if they don't name it. They're grieving the version of their parent who didn't need help.

**Premise 2:** The internet is saturated with generic "10 tips for choosing a care home" content. Nobody is short on checklists. What's scarce is someone who speaks to the emotional reality with honesty and without an agenda.

**Premise 3:** Content that genuinely helps people builds trust faster than content that sells. Trust converts to business at the highest rate. Therefore, the highest-ROI content strategy prioritizes genuine help over lead generation.

**Logical chain (hypothetical syllogism):**
If Becca creates the most genuinely helpful eldercare content in Washington (P→Q),
and genuinely helpful content builds the deepest trust (Q→R),
and deep trust converts to business at the highest rate (R→S),
then creating the most helpful content leads to the most business (P→S).

## Content Creation Rules

1. **Help first. Optimize second.** Write content so genuinely useful that people save it, share it with siblings, forward it to their spouse, and come back to it at 2am when they can't sleep.

2. **Speak to the grief.** Don't just address logistics. Address the emotional reality: guilt, fear, identity loss, family conflict, exhaustion, love.

3. **Use their words, not ours.** Source every piece from what real families are saying in support communities. Use their language, not clinical language.

4. **Be the person Becca needed.** Every article, every post, every sentence should pass the test: "Would this have helped Becca when she was going through this with her own parents?"

5. **Becca's voice, Becca's heart.** Compassionate, honest, human. Never corporate. Never salesy. She has a good sense of humor that can show through even in serious topics.

6. **Standalone value on every platform.** Social media posts should be genuinely helpful on their own, not just teasers for a blog post.

7. **SEO follows quality.** If the content is truly the best resource on a topic for Washington families, Google will rank it. We optimize structure (headings, schema, keywords) but never at the expense of genuine value.

## Daily Content Outputs

1. **One deep-dive article** (2,000+ words): Could genuinely change how a family thinks about their situation
2. **3-5 social media posts** (Facebook, Instagram, LinkedIn): Each standalone valuable
3. **1 "micro-moment" piece**: A single powerful sentence or short paragraph. The kind of thing someone screenshots and sends to their sister.

## The Test for Every Piece of Content

Before publishing, ask:
- Would a stressed adult daughter at 11pm find this genuinely helpful?
- Would she send this to her brother who thinks "Mom is fine"?
- Does this make Burien Best Care Home feel like a place of warmth and expertise, without us having to say so?
- Would Becca be proud to have her name on this?

If yes to all four, publish it.
