# Burien Best Care Home — Copywriting Framework

*Based on Dan Kennedy, Alex Hormozi, and Donald Miller StoryBrand research*

## The Combined Formula

1. **Kennedy's pain/urgency** — compelling headlines and deadline-driven CTAs
2. **Miller's empathetic storytelling** — families are heroes, facility is the guide
3. **Hormozi's value equation** — stack value so price objections disappear

## StoryBrand SB7 Applied to Burien Best Care Home

1. **Character (Hero):** The adult child (38-55) worried about their aging parent
2. **Problem:**
   - External: Parent declining, can't manage alone
   - Internal: Guilt, stress, overwhelm, fear of making the wrong choice
   - Philosophical: "Am I abandoning my parent?"
3. **Guide (Us):** Empathy ("We understand how hard this is") + Authority (20+ years, specialized staff)
4. **Plan:** Schedule tour → We assess needs → Family meets care team → Smooth transition
5. **CTA:** Direct: "Schedule a Visit" / Transitional: "Download Family Guide"
6. **Failure (Stakes):** Falls, medication errors, isolation, crisis decisions that cost more
7. **Success:** Parent thriving, family reunited without caregiving burden, peace of mind

## Homepage Structure

**Above the Fold (Kennedy + Miller):**
- Headline: Problem-Agitate-Solve
- Subheading: Empathy + solution
- Direct CTA: "Schedule Your Tour"
- Hero image: families/residents (candid, not posed)
- Trust markers: 20+ years, certifications

**The Family's Journey (Miller SB7):**
- Acknowledge their problem
- Show empathy + authority
- Present the simple plan (4 steps)
- Paint the success picture

**Value Stack (Hormozi):**
- All services listed with value framing
- Economic comparison to alternatives
- Testimonials as proof of dream outcome

**FAQ (Miller's 10 Doubts + Kennedy Urgency):**
- Address fears before they're voiced
- Include scarcity elements
- Social proof matching family situations

**CTA Finale (Kennedy + Miller):**
- Direct: "Schedule Your Free Tour"
- Transitional: "Download Our Family Guide"
- Urgency: "Tour availability is limited"

## Headline Formulas

- "[Problem + Agitate] + [Empathy] + [Outcome]"
- "Your parent's safety shouldn't keep you up at night"
- "Most families wait until a crisis. We help you decide before one happens."
- "Your parent's independence + your peace of mind. Everything else follows."

## ICP-Specific Messaging

**Adult Daughters (primary decision-maker, ~66% of eldercare decisions):**
- Emotional language: guilt, worry, wanting to do the right thing
- Searching at 11pm after a scary phone call
- Needs to feel like choosing care is LOVE, not abandonment

**Adult Sons (secondary, financial/logistics focus):**
- Practical language: costs, coverage, Medicaid, comparison to alternatives
- Needs clear value proposition and ROI framing
- Responds to authority signals and credentials

## Content Rules
- NEVER use em dashes
- Voice: compassionate, trustworthy, professional but deeply human
- Frame outcomes, not features ("peace of mind" not "24/7 care")
- PAS on every page section
- Gentle CTAs (not pushy)
